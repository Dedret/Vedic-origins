Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our A2 Ghee

About Our Product

What is A2 Ghee?
A2 Ghee is made from milk that contains only the A2 beta-casein protein, produced by indigenous cow breeds like Gir, Sahiwal, and Red Sindhi. This is the original type of milk that humans consumed for thousands of years before modern breeding introduced the A1 protein variant.
What is the Bilona method?
The Bilona method is a traditional technique where milk is first boiled and set to curd. The curd is then hand-churned using a wooden churner (bilona) in both clockwise and anticlockwise directions to extract butter. This butter is slowly heated on a wood fire to make Ghee. The entire process preserves nutrients and creates superior quality Ghee.
What makes your Ghee different from store-bought brands?
Our Ghee is 100% natural, made from A2 milk of free-grazing indigenous cows, hand-churned using the traditional Bilona method, and contains no preservatives or additives. Most commercial brands use machine-made processes, mixed-breed cow milk (A1+A2), and often add preservatives for shelf life.

Health & Benefits

What are the health benefits of A2 Ghee?
A2 Ghee is rich in vitamins A, D, E, and K, supports digestion, boosts immunity, provides healthy fats for brain function, helps in nutrient absorption, and has anti-inflammatory properties. It's easier to digest than A1 dairy and is suitable for lactose-intolerant individuals when consumed in moderation.
Is Ghee suitable for lactose-intolerant people?
Yes, pure Ghee contains very minimal lactose and casein as these proteins are removed during the clarification process. Most lactose-intolerant individuals can safely consume Ghee. However, if you have severe allergies, please consult your doctor first.

Storage & Usage

How should I store Ghee?
Store Ghee in an airtight container in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. You don't need to refrigerate it. Always use a clean, dry spoon to scoop out Ghee to prevent contamination. Properly stored Ghee can last for several months.
What is the shelf life of your Ghee?
Our Ghee has a shelf life of 12 months from the date of manufacture when stored properly. We recommend consuming it fresh for the best flavor and nutritional benefits.
How much Ghee should I consume daily?
For most adults, 1-2 teaspoons (5-10ml) per day is recommended. You can use it for cooking, add it to dal/rice, or spread it on rotis. Consult with a nutritionist or Ayurvedic practitioner for personalized recommendations.
